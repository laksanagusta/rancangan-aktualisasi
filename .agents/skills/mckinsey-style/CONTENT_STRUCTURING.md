# Content Structuring Guide

Step-by-step guide for transforming raw content into McKinsey-style structured presentation content.

---

## Step 1: Identify the Core Message

Before creating any slides, answer these questions:

1. **What is the single most important thing the audience should take away?**
   → This becomes your executive summary lead sentence.

2. **What decision do you want the audience to make?**
   → This becomes your conclusion/recommendation.

3. **What are the 3-5 key arguments supporting that decision?**
   → These become your body slide sections.

---

## Step 2: Apply the Pyramid

Structure content top-down:

```
[Core Recommendation]
    ├── [Argument 1]
    │     ├── [Evidence 1a]
    │     └── [Evidence 1b]
    ├── [Argument 2]
    │     ├── [Evidence 2a]
    │     └── [Evidence 2b]
    └── [Argument 3]
          ├── [Evidence 3a]
          └── [Evidence 3b]
```

### Mapping Pyramid to Slides

| Pyramid Level | Slide Location |
|---|---|
| Core Recommendation | Executive Summary + Conclusion |
| Arguments | Section headers / Action titles |
| Evidence | Body content (charts, bullets, data) |

---

## Step 3: Validate MECE

For each group of arguments, check:

### Mutually Exclusive Test
Ask: "Do any two arguments overlap in content or meaning?"
- If yes → merge or redefine boundaries

### Collectively Exhaustive Test
Ask: "Do all arguments together fully cover the recommendation? Is anything missing?"
- If no → identify the gap and add an argument

### Common MECE Patterns

| Category | Example Decomposition |
|---|---|
| By time | Past → Present → Future |
| By geography | Region A → Region B → Region C |
| By stakeholder | Customers → Employees → Shareholders |
| By function | Marketing → Operations → Finance |
| By impact | High priority → Medium → Low |
| By process | Input → Process → Output |

---

## Step 4: Write Action Titles

Transform each argument into a **complete sentence action title** for each slide.

### The Action Title Formula

```
[Subject] + [Verb] + [Key Data/Insight] + [Implication/Context]
```

### Examples by Slide Type

**Data Analysis Slides:**
- "Customer acquisition cost increased 45% YoY, outpacing revenue growth by 2x"
- "Southeast Asian markets show 3x higher engagement rates than mature markets"

**Recommendation Slides:**
- "Consolidating three platforms into one will reduce operational costs by $2.4M annually"
- "Hiring 50 additional engineers in Q2 is required to meet the product launch deadline"

**Process/Framework Slides:**
- "Four key capabilities must be developed to enable the digital transformation"
- "The current approval process has seven unnecessary handoffs that add 12 days of delay"

**Comparison Slides:**
- "Company's market share declined in all regions except APAC, which grew 15%"
- "Competitor A leads in price while Company leads in customer satisfaction and retention"

---

## Step 5: Structure the Storyline (SCR)

Map your content to the SCR framework:

### Situation Slides (1-2 slides)
- Current state of affairs
- Context the audience needs
- Why this topic matters now

**Typical action titles:**
- "The [industry] faces [challenge] as [trend] accelerates"
- "[Organization] has achieved [milestone] but faces [new challenge]"

### Complication Slides (2-4 slides)
- What has changed
- Challenges and obstacles
- Urgency factors
- Consequences of inaction

**Typical action titles:**
- "[Metric] has declined [X]% since [time], driven by [factors]"
- "Three structural barriers prevent [organization] from achieving [goal]"
- "Without intervention, [consequence] will occur by [timeframe]"

### Resolution Slides (3-5 slides)
- Recommended actions
- Expected outcomes
- Implementation roadmap
- Required resources

**Typical action titles:**
- "Implementing [strategy] will address [challenge] and generate [benefit]"
- "A phased approach over [timeframe] minimizes risk while delivering results"
- "Investment of [amount] is projected to yield [X]x return within [timeframe]"

---

## Step 6: Choose Layouts

For each slide in your storyline, select the most appropriate layout:

| Content Type | Recommended Layout | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| Single trend or data point | Layout A: Full Graph | Time series, market trends |
| Concept with visual explanation | Layout B: Infographic + Text | Process flows, frameworks |
| Detailed recommendations | Layout C: Full Text | Key findings, next steps |
| Comparisons or lists | Layout D: Text + Icons | Myths/realities, features |
| Multi-topic overview | Layout E: Executive Summary | Executive summary, dashboards |

### Layout Distribution Guidelines

A well-balanced McKinsey deck typically follows this distribution:

- **Title:** 1 slide
- **Executive Summary:** 1 slide (Layout E)
- **Situation:** 1-2 slides (Layout A or B)
- **Complication:** 2-4 slides (Layout A, B, or D)
- **Resolution:** 3-5 slides (Layout B, C, or D)
- **Conclusion/Next Steps:** 1 slide (Layout C)

**Total: 9-14 slides for a standard deck**

---

## Step 7: Write Body Content

### For Chart Slides (Layout A)
- Choose chart type that best tells the story
- Add subtitle with methodology note
- Include one callout annotation for the key insight
- Keep legend minimal

### For Text Slides (Layout C)
- Maximum 3 sections per slide
- Maximum 3-4 bullets per section
- Each bullet: **one idea, one breath**
- Bold the key term or number in each bullet
- No full sentences — fragments are fine for bullets

### For Comparison Slides (Layout D)
- Parallel structure between compared items
- Same level of detail for each item
- Use icons consistently
- Highlight the key differentiator

---

## Step 8: Add Sources

Every data point needs a source. Format:

```
Source: [Organization], [Publication/Report Name], [Year]
```

**Examples:**
- Source: McKinsey Global Institute, "Digital Transformation Report," 2024
- Source: Internal company data, Q3 FY2024 financial report
- Source: World Bank Open Data, GDP growth indicators, 2023

**Multiple sources on one slide:**
```
Sources: 1. World Bank (2023); 2. Internal survey data (n=2,400); 3. Industry benchmarks (Gartner, 2024)
```

## Content Transformation Example

### Before (Raw Content)
> "Our company has been struggling with customer churn lately. We've seen a big increase in the number of customers leaving over the past year. The main reasons seem to be poor customer service, lack of product updates, and competitors offering better prices. We think we should invest in customer success and improve our product."

### After (McKinsey-Structured)

**Executive Summary Action Title:**
"Customer churn increased 32% YoY, requiring a $1.5M investment in customer success and product acceleration to prevent $8M in annual revenue loss"

**Slide 1 (Situation) — Layout A:**
Title: "Annual customer base grew 2x in three years, but churn rate has accelerated beyond sustainable levels"
Body: Line chart showing customer growth vs. churn rate over 3 years

**Slide 2 (Complication) — Layout D:**
Title: "Exit interviews reveal three primary drivers of churn, all addressable within 6 months"
Body: Icons + text grid:
1. Service Response — Average ticket resolution time is 72hrs (industry benchmark: 24hrs)
2. Product Stagnation — Zero major feature releases in 9 months vs. 4 by top competitor
3. Price Pressure — Competitor launched tier 30% below our entry price

**Slide 3 (Resolution) — Layout C:**
Title: "A two-track investment in customer success and product acceleration will reduce churn by 40% within 12 months"
Body: Bulleted priorities with cost and expected impact

**Slide 4 (Next Steps) — Layout C:**
Title: "Immediate actions in Q1 will set the foundation for sustained retention improvement"
Body: 4 concrete next steps with owners and deadlines
